When This Calculator Is Useful

Use this calculator when you have received a job offer or contract proposal and need to understand how different worker classifications affect your federal take-home pay. It is especially helpful when comparing a 1099 contractor offer against a W-2 salaried position with the same gross amount.

Formula

W-2 take-home = gross wages − federal income tax − employee Social Security tax − employee Medicare tax.

1099 take-home = gross receipts − self-employment tax − federal income tax, where self-employment tax is calculated on 92.35% of net earnings and 50% of that tax is deducted before federal income tax is computed.

Calculation Methodology

For the W-2 side, the calculator starts with gross wages, subtracts the 2025 standard deduction for a single filer to determine taxable income, computes progressive federal income tax using 2025 bracket thresholds, and adds employee FICA taxes made up of 6.2% Social Security tax up to the wage base and 1.45% Medicare tax on all wages.

For the 1099 side, the calculator treats gross receipts as net earnings in version 1, multiplies those earnings by 92.35% to determine the self-employment tax base, calculates the Social Security and Medicare portions of self-employment tax, deducts 50% of the self-employment tax for income tax purposes, then applies the 2025 standard deduction and 2025 federal income tax brackets. It does not include state income taxes, itemized deductions, business expenses, QBI, credits, benefits, or filing-status variations.

FAQ

Does this include state tax?
No. This tool is federal-only.

Does it model business expenses?
No. Version 1 assumes net earnings equal gross receipts.

Does it use itemized deductions?
No. It uses the standard deduction only.
